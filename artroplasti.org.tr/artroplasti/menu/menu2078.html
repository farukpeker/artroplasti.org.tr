Why Membership?

The Hip and Knee Arthroplasty Association brings together specialists in orthopedics and traumatology focused on joint diseases and arthroplasty. Our aims are sharing scientific knowledge, continuity in education, patient safety, and professional solidarity.

What Membership Provides

📚 Scientific Community

Become part of a professional network where up-to-date arthroplasty literature and experience are shared.

🎓 Education & Courses

Priority announcements for basic/advanced arthroplasty courses, cadaver trainings, and congresses.

🧭 Contribution to Standards

Opportunity to help develop nationwide clinical practice standards.

🤝 Working Groups

Chance to serve in focus groups such as PJI, VTE, revision surgery, and robotic/navigation.

🌐 National / International Network

Connect with a broad network of experts through association events and collaborations.

🧪 Resources & E-Learning

One-stop access to e-learning content and links to current consensus statements.

Who Can Become a Member?

  • Full Membership: Orthopedics and Traumatology specialist physicians may be admitted upon a proposal by a full member and Board of Directors’ approval.
  • Honorary Membership: Individuals who have contributed scientifically/professionally to the field or offered support may become honorary members by Board of Directors’ decision.
  • Residents: Physicians in specialty training may participate in association activities; as specified in the bylaws, they do not have duties or voting rights in certain bodies.

For full conditions, please see the Association Bylaws and Membership Information pages.

Expectations from Members

  • Commitment to ethical principles and prioritizing patient safety.
  • Contribution to scientific exchange: presentations, publications, case/experience sharing.
  • Participation in educational activities and volunteering when needed.
  • Strengthening a culture of professional communication and collaboration.

Gains

Event announcements E-Learning access Scientific collaborations Contribution to standards Professional visibility
